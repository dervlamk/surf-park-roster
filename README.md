# DSRT Surf Park — Operations Package

This document explains the contents of `DSRT_Operations_Package.xlsx`, a complete operational planning workbook for DSRT Surf Park. It covers wave scheduling, staffing, lifeguard rotations, and daily duty rosters across all seven days of the week, built to California labor law requirements.

---

## How to open

Open `DSRT_Operations_Package.xlsx` in Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets. Each tab along the bottom is a separate planning document. Start with **Sheet 1 (Legend & Overview)** before using any of the rosters.

---

## Sheet-by-sheet guide

### Sheet 1 — Legend & Overview

The reference sheet for the entire workbook. Contains:

- **Operating hours** for each day of the week — park open/close times, first and last wave, and session count
- **Wave calendar colour code** — explains the three cell colours used in the Wave Calendar and duty rosters (yellow, purple, white)
- **Station code definitions** — every abbreviation used in the duty rosters (CTR, RR, LR, MEAL, REST, etc.) with a plain-language description of each post
- **California labor law break rules** — the meal and rest break requirements that all rosters are built around

Always refer back to this sheet if a code or colour is unclear.

---

### Sheet 2 — Wave Calendar (Optimised)

A weekly overview of every wave session across all seven days.

**Columns:** Monday through Sunday  
**Rows:** Each hourly time slot from 6am to 8pm (earlier on some days)

**Cell colours:**

| Colour | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Yellow | Beginner lesson eligible — land lesson starts 30 min before the wave slot; 6 coaches enter the water with students |
| Purple | Advanced session requiring an extra coach in the water if the group exceeds 10 people (Progressive Turns, Malibu, Barrel Hour) |
| White | No beginner lesson possible — intermediate or advanced surfers only |

**★ symbol** in a cell means a lesson is scheduled for that slot.

**Scheduling rules** (printed below the calendar):
- Beginner lessons cannot run back-to-back — minimum 2-hour gap between sessions
- The land component of a lesson starts 30 minutes before the wave slot begins
- 4pm is the primary weekday beginner window; weekends add a 10am window
- 6pm–8pm every day is Barrel Hour — prime viewing, no group lessons

**Headcount summary** at the bottom of the sheet:

| | Count |

### Sheet 4 — LG Rotation Template

Explains how the 60-minute lifeguard rotation works. This template applies to all shift types on all days.

**The 8 stations (in rotation order):**

| Code | Post |
|------|------|
| CTR | Control Tower |
| RR | Right Reef |
| RS | Right Shore |
| FLT | Floater |
| LR | Left Reef |
| LS | Left Shore |
| FAR | First Aid Room |
| BR | Board Rental |

Each guard starts at a different position in this cycle, so all 8 posts are covered simultaneously at any given moment. Every 60 minutes, everyone rotates one step forward.

**Guard-1 and Guard-2 start at reef stations (RR and LR)** — these must be surfer-qualified guards.

**Meal breaks** are staggered: Guard-1 takes their 30-minute unpaid meal break first, then Guard-2, and so on. The Relief Guard covers the vacated post during each meal window.

**Rest breaks** (10 minutes, paid) are taken within the 60-minute rotation slot at approximately the 3.5-hour mark — guards step away briefly while still being counted in the rotation.

The break schedule table at the bottom of the sheet shows the specific rest break times and meal windows for each of the 8 guards plus the Relief Guard.

### Sheet 5 — Duty Roster

The full-day duty roster for Monday through Thursday.

**Time range:** 7:00am – 8:30pm (27 half-hour rows)  
**Structure:** Two overlapping shifts

| Shift | Hours | Staff |
|-------|-------|-------|
| **Shift A** (blue columns) | 7:00am – 3:30pm | Supervisor-A, 2 Senior LGs (reef), 5 LGs, Relief-A |
| **Shift B** (orange columns) | 12:00pm – 8:30pm | Supervisor-B, 2 LGs (reef), 3 LGs, 2 Coaches, Board Rental, Relief-B |

**Shift overlap:** 12:00pm–3:30pm both shifts are on-site simultaneously, providing maximum coverage during peak afternoon hours.

**Group lesson team** (shown separately at the bottom): 4–6 additional coaches called in specifically for the 4pm lesson window (3:00pm–6:30pm). These are separate from Shift A and B and only work on lesson days.

**Key events in the schedule:**
- 4:00pm — Beginner group lesson (land prep at 3:30pm, in-water 4:00pm–5:00pm)
- 5:00pm onward — Coaches switch to private coaching on Left and Right Reef
- 6:00pm–8:00pm — Barrel Hour (advanced/pro sessions, no group lessons)
- 8:30pm — Debrief and close

**Reading a row:** Each column shows one staff member's assignment for that 30-minute slot. Cell colour and code indicate the station (e.g. blue = CTR, red = MEAL, green = GRP-LES). "OFF" in grey means that person's shift has not yet started or has ended.

## California labor law compliance

All rosters are designed to meet the following requirements:

- **Meal break:** 1 × 30-minute unpaid break for any shift over 5 hours, taken before the end of the 5th hour
- **Rest break:** 1 × 10-minute paid break for every 3.5 hours worked (8.5-hour shifts include two rest breaks)
- **Second meal break:** Required for shifts exceeding 10 hours — no shifts in this plan reach that threshold
- All breaks are staggered across guards so minimum staffing levels are maintained at all times
