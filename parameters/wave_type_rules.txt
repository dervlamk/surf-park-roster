Wave Schedule & Work Duty Schedule
 1. Wave types are in broad 3 categories: Progressive, Good, Shredder and reflect increasing skill ability.
 2. Progressive Wave types: 
    a.	Malibu,
    b.	Progressive Turns
 3. Good Wave Types:
    a.	Intermediate
    b.	Intermediate +
    c.	Intermediate Challenger
 4. Shredder Wave Types
    a.	Advanced Turns
    b.	Advanced Turns +
    c.	Advanced Challenger
    d.	Pro Challenger
 5. Beginner Lessons occur in the bay and require 3 coaches per lagoon side, a total of 6.
 6. Progressive wave types require 1 coach per lagoon side if more than 10 people book a session
 7. Beginner Lessons cannot run back to back as there is a 30 minute land lesson at the beginning of the lesson.
 8. Beginner Lesson is 90 minutes long (30 minutes land lesson and 60 minutes in the pool)
 9. I will need a calculator/tracking system of the schedule to give me a % of the wave types scheduled.
10. Possible operating hours are from 6am-12am (18 hours) on Monday-Sunday.
12. Managed Progressive sessions can be performed by a Reef Guard or a Bay Coach.
13. The goal of the schedule and the duty roster is to be optimized to save payroll cost and to have ideal flow throughout the day with reduced head count.
15. Opening shift of any kind begins 30 minutes prior to the waves starting. Shifts after the morning shift have a 30minute overlap with the outgoing shift, which give them 15 minutes on the clock to get organized before rotating into the life guard rotation. Advanced coaches do not have 30 minute overlap.
